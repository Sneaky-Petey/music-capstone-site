Conclusion

What I Found

  • The effect of music depends on the task and the person.
  • Instrumental music at moderate volume often supported focus.
  • Lyrical music was more distracting during reading and writing tasks.

Limitations

  • Small sample size.
  • Different music preferences and familiarity.
  • Mood, sleep, stress, and distractions are hard to control.

Improvements for Next Time

  • Use more participants and repeated trials.
  • Track volume more carefully.
  • Separate data by task type (math, reading, memorization).

Recommendation

A safe study choice is instrumental music at low or moderate volume, then switching to silence for heavy reading, writing, or memorization.
